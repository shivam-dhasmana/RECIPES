BIRYANI

A royal blend of aromatic rice, exotic spices, and tender ingredients, slow-cooked to create a symphony of flavors in every bite

Biryani is a fragrant and flavorful rice dish that combines aromatic basmati rice with richly spiced vegetables or meat, slow-cooked to perfection. Originating from the Indian subcontinent, it is known for its layered cooking style, where rice, spices, and marinated ingredients blend together, infusing every bite with deep, complex flavors.

Often garnished with fried onions, fresh coriander, and saffron-infused milk, biryani is a celebration of taste, texture, and aroma—making it a beloved dish for festive occasions, family gatherings, or simply a hearty, comforting meal.

INGRIDIENS FOR BIRYANI

  • 2 cups basmati rice (soaked 30 min
  • 2 tbsp ghee or oil
    • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrot, beans, peas, potato, capsicum)
  • 1 large onion (sliced)
  • 1 large tomato (chopped)
  • 2–3 green chilies (slit)
  • 1 cup curd (yogurt)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • Fresh coriander & mint leaves (chopped)
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tbsp red chili powder
  • 2 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Whole spices (2 bay leaves, 4 cardamoms, 4 cloves, 1 cinnamon stick)
  • Saffron strands in 2 tbsp warm milk (optional)

LETS START

Step 1: cook rice

  1. Boil water with salt and a few whole spices.
  2. Add soaked basmati rice and cook till 80% done. Drain and keep aside.

Step 2: prepare masala

  1. Heat ghee/oil in a pan.
  2. Add whole spices, then sliced onion, and sauté till golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté 1 minute.
  4. Add tomatoes, turmeric, red chili, coriander powder, and salt. Cook till tomatoes soften.
  5. Add vegetables, curd, and mix well. Cook for 5–6 minutes.
  6. Sprinkle garam masala and fresh coriander-mint leaves

Step 3: layer the biryani

  1. In a heavy-bottom pan, spread one layer of rice, then a layer of veg masala
  2. Repeat and finish with rice on top
  3. Drizzle saffron milk (optional)

Step 4: Serve

  1. Garnish with a swirl of cream and a few coriander leaves.
  2. Gently mix and serve hot with raita or salad
