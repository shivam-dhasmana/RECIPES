PANEER-BUTTER-MASALA

Paneer Butter Masala is a rich and creamy North Indian curry made with soft paneer cubes simmered in a luscious tomato-cashew gravy. Flavored with aromatic spices, fresh cream, and a touch of butter, this mildly spiced dish is a perfect balance of sweetness and tang. Best enjoyed with naan, roti, or steamed rice

Paneer Butter Masala is one of the most popular vegetarian dishes in Indian cuisine, loved for its rich, creamy texture and flavorful gravy. This restaurant-style curry features soft cubes of paneer cooked in a silky smooth tomato-based sauce enriched with butter, cream, and a blend of aromatic spices. The subtle sweetness of cashews balances the tanginess of tomatoes, while the addition of garam masala and kasuri methi brings out the authentic North Indian flavor. It’s a comforting, indulgent dish perfect for family dinners, special occasions, or when you’re simply craving something delicious

To make this mouthwatering curry, start by sautéing onions, tomatoes, ginger, garlic, and cashews, then blend them into a smooth paste. Cook this paste in butter with spices until fragrant, then add paneer cubes and fresh cream to create a luscious gravy. Garnish with coriander leaves and a swirl of cream for a restaurant-style presentation. Serve hot with naan, roti, or steamed basmati rice for a truly satisfying meal that’s bound to impress everyone at the table.

INGRIDIENS FOR PANEER-BUTTER-MASALA

  • Paneer (cottage cheese) – 200-250 g (cut into cubes)
  • Butter – 2 tbsp
    • Oil – 1 tbsp
  • Onion – 1 medium (roughly chopped)
  • Tomatoes – 3 large (roughly chopped)
  • Cashew nuts – 8-10 (optional, for creaminess)
  • Ginger – 1 inch piece
  • Garlic – 4-5 cloves
  • Green chilli – 1 (optional, for spice)
  • Red chilli powder – 1 tsp
  • Turmeric powder – ¼ tsp
  • Coriander powder – 1 tsp
  • Garam masala – ½ tsp
  • Kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves) – 1 tsp
  • Fresh cream – 2-3 tbsp
  • Salt – to taste
  • Sugar or honey – 1 tsp (optional, to balance flavors)
  • Water – as needed
  • Fresh coriander leaves – for garnish

LETS START

Step 1: Prepare the base gravy

  1. Heat 1 tbsp oil + 1 tbsp butter in a pan.
  2. Add chopped onions and sauté until soft and light golden.
  3. Add garlic, ginger, and green chili. Sauté for 1–2 minutes until aromatic.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cashews. Cook until tomatoes turn soft.
  5. Add ½ tsp turmeric, 1 tsp coriander powder, and 1 tsp Kashmiri red chili powder. Mix well.
  6. Turn off the heat and let it cool slightly.
  7. Blend into a smooth paste using a little water. This is your masala base

Step 2: Cook the gravy

  1. In the same pan, heat 1 tbsp butter.
  2. Pour in the blended masala paste and cook on medium heat for 4–5 minutes until it thickens and releases oil from the sides.
  3. Add salt, sugar, and garam masala. Stir well.
  4. Add ½–1 cup water (adjust consistency as you like) and bring to a gentle boil.

Step 3: Add paneer and finish

  1. Add paneer cubes to the gravy and cook for 2–3 minutes on low flame.
  2. Crush and add kasuri methi for that restaurant-style aroma.
  3. Stir in fresh cream to give a rich, creamy texture.

Step 4: Serve

  1. Garnish with a swirl of cream and a few coriander leaves.
  2. Serve hot with naan, roti, or jeera rice

SPECIAL TIPS

  1. Soak paneer cubes in warm water for 10 minutes before adding to keep them soft.
  2. Using Kashmiri red chili powder gives color without making it too spicy.
  3. Always crush kasuri methi between your palms before adding—it enhances aroma.
